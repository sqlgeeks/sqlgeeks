Normalisation

- Normalisation is a way of orgainising the data in the database so as to reduce or eliminate data redundancy from a relation.The different forms of Normalisation are : -

a. First Normal Form (1 NF)

- A relation R is in 1 NF iff there is no Multi-valued attributes in the relation.
- Every attribute of relation R must be atomic (single valued).

Ex: A relation R is defined as below -
S_ID S_Name C_ID
S1 A C1 / C2
S2 B C2 / C3
S3 B C3

The above relation is not in 1NF since it contains multivalued attributes. It can corrected as below so as to satisfy the 1 NF condition.

S_ID S_Name C_ID
S1 A C1
S1 A C2
S2 B C2
S2 B C3
S3 B C3

- Degree of redundancy is very high in 1 NF

a. Second Normal Form (2 NF)
  • A relation R is in 2 NF iff there is no Partial Dependencies in R.
  • A Partial Dependency is said to exists if "proper subset of a candidiate key is dependent on the non-prime attribute"

    • a. Third Normal Form (3 NF)
  • A relation R is in 3 NF iff every non-trivial Functional Dependency X -> Y in R with -
    a.   X must be a Super Key.
    • OR
    b.   Y must be a prime attribute.
  • In simple terms,
    • a.  [Super Key] -> Prime/Non Prime Attributes
    b.  [Not Super Key] -> Prime Attributes.

    a. Boyce Codd Normal Form (BCNF)
  • A relation R is in 3 NF iff every non-trivial Functional Dependency X -> Y in R with X must be a Super Key.
  • In simple terms, [Super Key] -> Prime/Non Prime Attributes
